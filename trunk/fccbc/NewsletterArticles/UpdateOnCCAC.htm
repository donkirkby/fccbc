Update on CCAC

By Bonnie Wong, FCC BC

CCAA (China Centre of Adoption Affairs) has recently announced a quota system for some countries including the United States, only allowing a certain number of adoption applications each year from each adoption agency and applications from single applicants allowed no more than 5% of the total quota. The quota became effective on December 1, 2001. Meanwhile, CCAA has decided not to impose a quota on English-speaking provinces in Canada at the present time. However for the year 2002, CCAA hopes that the provinces would not send more adoption applications to CCAA than what were sent in the year 2001, and applications from single applicants would be allowed no more than 5% of the total applications. For BC applicants, this change should not affect married applicants, and it should not affect single applicants whose dossiers were registered by CCAA before December 1, 2001.

Timing over at CCAA (from the time the applicants' dossier is registered by CCAA until the time CCAA is ready to match a child to the applicants) is unfortunately getting longer and longer. Currently the timing is about 13 months. Hopefully the quota system would be able to help stopping the timing getting longer.

CCAA's website is http://www.china-ccaa.org/

Bonnie Wong runs Tribo Services, a consultation agency facilitating adoptions from China.
