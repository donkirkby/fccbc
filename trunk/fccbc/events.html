Lotus Gala, October 17 2006

A special evening in aid of Half the Sky Foundation

  • Tuesday October 17, 2006 6:15pm - 10:00pm
  • Pink Pearl Restaurant 1132 E. Hastings St., Vancouver
  • Silent auction - fabulous prizes
  • Entertainment by the Orchid Ensemble
  • Presentation by Yves Maurais, founder of HTS Canada
  • 9-course Chinese dinner

Come join us for an evening of celebration and appreciation of the work of Half the Sky Foundation, a charity dedicated to providing education and enrichment programs for children in orphanages in China. Help make a difference in their lives.

A fun night out for parents with a 9-course Chinese dinner and silent auction. Lots of fabulous items to bid on! Live music entertainment and a presentation by Yves Maurais, founder of Half the Sky Foundation-Canada. Master of ceremonies: Fred Lee, CBC Radio's man about town.

Tickets are $45 each. Call the Adoptive Families Association of BC at (604)320-7730 or e-mail .

The Lotus Gala is also supported by AFABC, Family Services of Greater Vancouver, Sunrise Family Services and The Adoption Centre.

Annual General Meeting, November 4th 2006

10 am - 12 noon with lunch to follow

  • Queensborough Community Centre
  • 920 Ewen Avenue
  • New Westminster (Need directions? See Google maps.)
  • Special Guest Ivan Sayers: Chinese influence on 20th century fashion

Come to our AGM at the Queensborough Community Centre, same vicinity as the summer picnic. The AGM is a vital occasion to review accomplishments, acknowledge volunteers and set future direction. During the meeting, the younger children will be under supervised care in the daycare area, while the older ones busy themselves with crafts and other activities. After which, it's lunch for all and a fashion show. Those planning to attend, please rsvp Danielle (see above).

Winter Skating Party, December 23rd, 2006

  • 3:30 pm - 5:30 pm at Trout Lake ice rink
  • Trout Lake Recreation Complex
  • 3350 Victoria Drive, Vancouver, B.C. (Need directions? See Google maps.)

FCCBC is hosting a winter skating party for member families. The rink will be decorated as a winter fantasy. Attendance is limited to 200 people and is free to FCCBC members. Non-members are asked to make a donation. Skate rentals will be available at the rink for $2.40 per pair.
