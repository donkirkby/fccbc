Dear Friends: Why Do We Need the FCC?

From the day in July, 1991, I met my daughter in the orphanage, I knew I owed it to her to make a peer group for her. She has made peer groups of her own, of school friends, baseball and soccer team-mates and the like and she always will. But she will likely also need, someday, to reach out to other adoptees from China - to give and receive understanding and encouragement - and of course, Guangsi.

Close FCC friends have told me that they enjoy a small group of FCC friends, but see little need for an organization. It is true, small groups of parents who feel a close bond because they went to China together or something else, do enjoy natural and spontaneous ties. But people drift away and groups can easily dissolve over time. And what specifically will attract one of our grown children to another FCC grown child might have little to do with families we parents enjoy the company of in 1998.

We FCC members are made up of all sorts of people with different backgrounds and circumstances. We may have little else in common. But we do share the desire to bring up happy, healthy and confident children.

David Robinson and I are stepping down as co-chairs at the end of 1998 and I hope to pass on the editorship of the newsletter to someone soon. But we will continue to be very active in maintaining our FCC. In the best interests of all of our children, can I appeal to you to get involved and do the same?

Best wishes,

John Bowen
