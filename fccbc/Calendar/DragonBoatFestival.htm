Dragon Boat Festival - Vancouver

This festival is a lot of fun. The main event is of course the Dragon Boat races themselves, that take place in False Creek. But there is also various entertainments offered in the Plaza of Nations, and in the park beside.

The event commemorates the death of Qu Yuan, a poet and minister who lost influence through the intrigues of his enemies. He was so upset at the turn the court he served took, that he threw himself into a river. The local people, knowing he was a good man, rowed their boats out to try and find him.

For more information about the historical and cultural meanings of the festival, and the web site for the Vancouver event, see our Dragon Boat Festival Links.

The Festival usually runs in the third week of June, and lasts for the entire weekend. In 2002 the event goes from June 21st to 23rd.
