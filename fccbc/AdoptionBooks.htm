Books On Adoption That You Can Buy from the AFA

Raising Healthy Multiracial Adoptive Families by the APABC. 72 page booklet set up in an easy-to-read question-and-answer format and addresses such topics as Sharing Your Story, Culture and Heritage, Speaking Up, School Issues, Stereotypes, and Racism. Also included is a detailed glossary and bibliography. $12.00

Through Moon and Stars and Night Skies by Ann Turner. Soft illustrations lend this tale of a little boy and his international adoption story a warm welcoming feel. A meaningful introduction to adoption. $9.95

Did My First Mother Love Me? by Kathryn Ann Miller. Aimed at the young, adopted child wondering about his or her birth parents. A good jumping off point for adoption discussions. $7.50

Let's Talk About Adoption by Fred Rogers of television fame. Explains adoption through photos and a story. Multiracial. $9.25

When The Sun Kisses the Sea by Susan Gabel. This wonderful story, highlighted by textured water colours, tells of a boy's memories of his birth country. $15.50

Susan and Gordon Adopt a Baby Introduces pre-schoolers to adoption at a level they can understand. From the folks at Sesame Street. $14.95

Real For Sure Sister by Ann Angel. This honest and heartfelt novel looks at family love and growing up adopted in a mixed race family. $8.95

We're Different, We're the Same by Bobbi Lane Kates. The folks at Sesame Street humorously point out our superficial differences while stressing the universalities that bind us all

The Colour of My Fur by Nanette Brophy. This direct, emotional story has orange bunnies battling purple bunnies simply because their fur is different. Non-violent. $12.95.

Who Cares About Race and Colour? by Child's Play. Clearly illustrates for children why we can all get along regardless of colour. $6.95

Multiracial Dolls Asian, Latino and African dolls in various shapes and sizes, from Barbie-type dolls to cloth dolls. $9.95 to $18.95

Books On China That You Can Buy from the AFA

Chin Chiang and the Dragon Dance by Ian Wallace. Delicate water-colours accent this story of a boy's first experience dancing the dragon's dance at the New Year's parade. $7.25

Lon Po Po: A Red-Riding Hood Story From China by Ed Young. This Caldecott medal winner is illustrated with pastel and water colours to tell this ancient folk tale. $21.50

Lion Dance Ernie Wan's Chinese New Year by Kate Waters and Madeleine Slovenz-Low. Photographs by Martha Cooper. This Reading Rainbow book tells the photographic story of Emie Wan's first Lion Dance on the streets of New York. $4.95

The Girl Who Loved Caterpillars adapted by Lean Merrill. This courtly tale of 12th Century JAFAn tells the story of a free-spirited, strong-willed young girl who prefers caterpillars, worms and toads to the refined hobbies of her peers. $7.95 Ages 9-13.

Yeh-Shen: A Cinderella Story from China retold by Ai-Ling-Louie and illustrated by Ed Young. This poetic Chinese Cinderella tale may be the precursor to the European version of the oft told fairy tale. Its misty illustrations lend dream-like quality to this lovely tale. $7.75

Chinese New Year's Dragon by Rachel Sing. Describes rituals of Chinese New Year's through the words of a young girl. $6.50

The Emperor and the Kite by lane Yolen. A magical Chinese tale about how a tiny daughter's courage and loyalty saves her father's life. Illustrated with Oriental paper cuts.

The Five Chinese Brothers by Claire Huchet Bishop and Kurt Wiese. First published in 1938, this classic tale tells the story of five identical brothers who outwit a judge and escape an undeserved death.

Chinese Proverbs by Ruthanne Lum McCunn. This pocket-sized hard-backed offers translations of famous Chinese proverbs from, "The eyes are wide, but the stomach is narrow," to "a lean dog shames its master." Written in Chinese characters and English. $10.95

When You Were Born in China This moving photo-essay provides a child's eye view of Chinese adoptions, helping to explain some of the "whys" and "hows" that have brought these children to their new families. $22.50

Count Your Way through China by Jim Haskins. Clever use of numbers treat readers to a brief introduction to culture, geography, and language. $8.50

The Moon Lady by Amy Tan. Rich, detailed art work illustrate this haunting of the Chinese Moon Festival. $7.95

The Nightingale by Michael Bedard Regolo Ricci. Text and illustrations combine to make this book and adventurous and appealing one, which offers the young readers a wonderful taste of Chinese past. $8.95

The Empty Pot Like a classic Chinese fable, this delicately crafted book has a simple moral and an honest, young hero. $8.95

The Weave of a Dream by Marilee Heyer Sumptuous illustrations accent a rich and intriguing tale of a widow who decides to weave a brocade into a glorious palace. $5.99

The Great Wall of China by Leonard Everett Fisher. This historical account of the building of the Great Wall of China is a testament to the strength and will of the Chinese people. $7.95

Chinese Mother Goose Rhymes Children of all stripes will enjoy this enchanting collection of poems, rhymes, and songs from the Chinese oral tradition-- Chinese Mother Goose. $10.95
