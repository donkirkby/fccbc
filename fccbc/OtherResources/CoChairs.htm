Our Two New Co-Chairs

Frank Kenny is a newcomer on the block. He and his wife, Monique, have forwarded their adoption papers to China and are waiting to complete the adoption process.

Frank is a retired neuropsychologist from Toronto who is presently enjoying his newfound freedom. His many past times include writing the music and lyrics for pop songs and playing these on his keyboard. Unfortunately his talents do not include a good singing voice, so he is on the look out for budding singing talent! He is recording his songs in New Brunswick.

Frank brings a new dynamic to FCC as a waiting adoptive parent. He is part of a waiting parents group and will be encouraging their participation in FCC events as well as seeking active involvement by FCC members in helping waiting parents go through the adoption process.

David Robinson is an old timer who has been a past co-chair of FCC as well as a Director of the Adoptive Families Association of BC. He is a litigation lawyer, with emphasis on personal injury, in his own small firm in Richmond. He is still involved in the notarization of adoption papers of adopting parents going to China.

David and his wife Jackie, have two adorable girls adopted from China, Cleone (4) and Maia (6) and Jackie is a contented stay at home mom.

Maia fell victim to Frank’s lovely humour when she first met him. Uncle Frank suggested that she give him three smarties and Maia being somewhat generous gave him three small packets of smarties in return for $20! Uncle Frank drew a cheque for $20 on the bank of Maia, purportedly signed by David Robinson as president of the bank! Maia was not fooled a bit and has given her dad strict instructions to recover full damages from Uncle Frank.

Unfortunately due to David’s other commitments, he can only give limited time to FCC and has indicated that despite the two-year tenure as co-chair, he may resign after one year. He hopes by that time FCC will be heading in a new direction and there will be members clamouring to take over as co-chair.

Plans for FCC

It is too early for the co-chairs to say what their plans are, but following the AGM, when positive support and ideas were given, they can give some indication of where they intend taking FCC with the support of its members. There will be a drive for new membership at both ends of the spectrum, waiting parents as well as parents with older children. To achieve this, the co-chairs will be promoting better communication both with and between members, and new programs/events of interest to both young and old children.

John Bowen, another former co-chair, has offered to liaise with the Chinese Cultural Centre, and help design the Chinese Cultural Camp for 2002. Wanda Pierson helped shape this camp last year for our children and it was well received by those who attended. The intention is to have this camp in July/August (or if sufficient interest is shown, at other times as well) at the Chinese Cultural Centre with emphasis for children probably in the 4 to 9 year age group. There will be more published about the proposed camps in the New Year. FCC seeks your support by having your children attend this camp.

The newsletter is going to be revitalized by Kathy Stewart. It is vitally important that FCC be kept informed on what is happening with its members and their children. FCC wants to know, for example, how waiting adopting parents can be helped, what problems are encountered in the adoption process, what Chinese schools children are attending, what adoption issues are coming up, particularly with older children, how these are being handled, how FCC can help in any way and all the myriad matters that new adopting parents will want to know from those who have older children. Your support is very much needed.

Rick Waugh is continuing to run the FCC website. He is putting a discussion line on the website. Hopefully this will be invaluable for waiting parents seeking information and contacting members who have recently returned from China. The discussion line can be used to inform others of schools, daycares, Chinese teachers, and Chinese cultural events in localities where members live. Please try out the discussion line as soon as possible.

Wanda Pierson, the previous co-chair for FCC, has kindly agreed to run the Chinese New Year event on February 2, 2002 for the third (and understandably the last) time. Danielle Soulodre will be assisting Wanda. This event is quite an undertaking and help is required to make it a complete success. Wanda does have a number of volunteers already, but more are needed. Please give a little time to volunteer for this event. We depend on members to make this event the success it is. Remember that early registration is required for this event, so please book now if you have not already done so.

Also needed are one or more individuals to take over the running of the Chinese New Year event for 2003 and those interested are requested to contact either Frank or David. They invite feedback on whether this event should be held yearly in its current form at the CCC, which requires a lot of planning and manpower. Alternatives, of a less demanding nature, may include having the event held at a hotel or suitable restaurant.

The Chinese New Year parade in Chinatown will be held on February 17, 2002 which is the Sunday following Chinese New Year.

Lorna Hunter is the liaison person in charge of the Volunteer Chinese Grandparents Project. For those who do not know about this, this is an undertaking promoted with the assistance of the Chinese Cultural Centre in which families are matched with a Chinese senior. The senior shares experiences with you and your children.

Cathy McDormand continues as FCC’s treasurer. Peter Gibson continues to be in charge of membership and FCC’s Directory.

Heather McDonald will be co-ordinating the summer picnic, which has been booked for July 7, 2002 at Ryall Park Picnic Shelter, New Westminster. Please mark your calendars for this event. Last year was a wonderful hot summer day and the water park, playground and clown was a great hit with the children. Entertainment will also be arranged for this picnic.

Camille Gueymard is looking into booking the North Vancouver Outdoor School facilities in Paradise Valley near Squamish for a weekend either in spring or summer. The facilities have huts and chalets for 20 to 80 people. The idea is to have an event where parents and children can spend time together, and socialize. It is hoped such an event would appeal to those with older children. Activities may include horseback riding and other outdoor pursuits. David Robinson also has in mind organizing a soccer game between older children and parents!

Steve Whan is researching the feasibility of setting up a website for North America linked to the FCC website, which would be for FCC members wishing to offer other members, home exchanges, rentals to and hosting of other FCC members. Such a site would make it possible for members and their children to stay with or meet up with other FCC members when they travel. This was brought home to David Robinson when he met a lady from the US at last year’s FCC picnic who had traveled to Vancouver for the CCC cultural camp and had rented an expensive and far from satisfactory apartment.

FCC last year arranged for one of its members to host an information session at their home for adopting parents about to go to China. It was an opportunity for people to get to know each other and ask questions in an informal setting. Speakers included Dr Elizabeth Watts who spoke about medical issues, Connie Munro (then co-chair) who spoke about her recent trip to adopt from China and David Robinson, who spoke about FCC. FCC will continue arranging one or two information sessions a year. Anyone interested in hosting a session are asked to contact David Robinson or Frank Kenny.

The co-chairs welcome all ideas to strengthen and foster ties between members and their children. Please write to the co-chairs and, if possible, be prepared to volunteer your services! FCC can only function successfully if its members volunteer their help.

Lastly FCC’s bank balance stands at a healthy sum of $7,788.
