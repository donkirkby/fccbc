UPDATE FROM THE MILLS FAMILY - February 23, 2000

Our faith in humanity has been restored!!

Over the past several weeks, our family has been blessed with support from kind-hearted souls. In many cases, the support has been from people we don't even know. Case in point: a benefit concert was held in Kim's honour on February 17th. It was organized, almost single-handedly, by a Kelowna musician whom we had never met. The only connection we have, is his daughter and Kimberley are in the same Kindergarten class.

The concert was a huge success and it's all because of this angel, disguised as a man. Actually, we are surrounded by angels, from family and friends and friends of theirs, to complete strangers. And, we would like to say from the bottom of our hearts - thank you for thinking of and caring for Kimberley.

The Provincial Tumour Board has reviewed Kim's case and has concluded that two chemotherapy sessions are not enough to gauge whether or not this is the route to go. The board figures it is still worthwhile pursuing chemo treatment, but have selected new drugs that won't be as hard on her hearing, as the previous chemo. The plan is to give three rounds of this new treatment, do a cat-scan to see if the tumour has shrunk, then perhaps follow up with a couple more treatment sessions. If the tumour shrinks, the next step is to remove it. If it doesn't shrink, radiation will be considered. Kim has undergone the first session (Feb.10-13) and is doing so much better than the other times. Her energy is pretty darn good and she's not experiencing the nausea or pain that was previously evident. In fact, we think she's doing better now, than before the diagnosis. We believe there are a few reasons, including the fact she has gained some weight back, thanks to the feeding tube, and also, due to the Chinese herbal supplement (Naturin 2) she's been taking. It's designed to boost the immune system. We think it must be working.

Kimberley even ventured off to school for a few days prior to going to B.C. Children's Hospital for the latest round of chemotherapy. She didn't miss a beat and her classmates were so good to her. It was sweet.

Kim undergoes her next round of chemo on March 02 and shortly after her return, will actually have something to look forward to---the trip to Disney World, organized and paid for by the Make-A-Wish Foundation. The timing should be such, that she ought to be feeling strong when we go.

It's wonderful to watch children adapt and incorporate challenges into their playtime. Kimmy and her sister, KayLee, often play "doctor" these days, and recently, Kim, playing the role of the doctor, told KayLee, the patient, that she would be right back---"I just have to consult with another doctor", she said. Oh, how they learn!

Again, thank you for caring about Kimberley!!!

David O. Mills
