FROM THE MILLS FAMILY December 26, 1999

If I had the opportunity at the orphanage to do it over…..I wouldn’t change a thing!

A little over 5 years ago (Nov.02/94), my wife and I were blessed when a 10 month old girl was placed in our arms. Here we were, standing worlds away, in an orphanage in the city of Zhanjiang, Guangdong province, People’s Republic of China, and a young, innocent life was placed in our trust and care. Moments before getting the pleasure of holding this little treasure, another of the many couples in our adoption group, had bent down to pick up this same infant girl, thinking it was their adoptive child. But the group facilitator (Bonnie Wong) was quick to point out the error. I often wonder, if the mistake had gone unnoticed, what would life be like today…for her, for us, for them. And I think about that a lot these days, because this little treasure that we call Kimberley WeiXiang Marie Mills, has recently (Nov.23rd) been diagnosed with cancer. Officially, it’s pancreatal blastoma…cancer of the pancreas. We are told it is a rare tumor, as far as children go, and in a rare location (behind the pancreas, as opposed to in front of it). As a result, the doctors say they aren’t in a position to give an accurate prognosis. Certainly, the hope is to shrink the tumour with chemotherapy treatment, then surgically remove it and follow up with more chemo. So, it’s one day at a time. Obviously, my wife and I are praying for our girl’s recovery so that she can continue to live out the dreams she has, and perhaps, even some of the ones we have envisioned. Again, I often think of that mistake at the orphanage, and then I smile and beam with pride, because in those 5 years, our girl has given us so much joy (as I hope and think we have given her). Bias perhaps, but we believe Kimberley to be extremely intelligent, with a soft demeanor, a good nature, a big heart, and a knockout smile that lights up a room. Truly, we have been blessed and we are better people for knowing this girl, this angel. And, in spite of the threat facing this young life, this sweet girl, if I had the opportunity at the orphanage to do it over….I WOULDN’T CHANGE A THING!!!

David O. Mills
