FCC from a Waiting Parent’s Perspective

By Frank Kenny, Co-chair, FCC BC & NWWA

A little over a year ago, my wife and I started to take the first steps towards adopting a child from China – steps that most of you will have long ago taken, and the tension of which you have perhaps even forgotten a little. Despite what has now already seemed like an eternity, we are still looking at another 6 months or more of waiting and the ‘final event’ still seems somewhat unreal.

Fortunately, one evening not too long after our initial ‘vetting’, we met a very resourceful and resolute woman at a Sunrise meeting, by the name of Morgan Armstrong. Morgan was also feeling a bit intimidated by the looming long wait and soon decided to form a resource group for parents waiting for children from China. For the five couples and one single who have met regularly since then, this has been a godsend. The waiting process has seemed much more bearable and through ‘special guest’ appearances we have also learned a great deal more about the process, what to expect, what causes delays and so on.

One of those guest appearances was from your immediate past co-chairman who promptly enlisted my participation as your next co-chair – to serve, fortunately, along with the long-experienced FCC co-founder, David Robinson. While accepting such a post seemed to fit well with my current ‘retiree’ status – that is, I thought some time to donate -- I certainly felt unsure of taking such a position, given that we had yet to accomplish our first adoption, compared to most of you who have had one or more children for some years. Nevertheless, as hands seemed to be needed, here I am. I hope that I can do credit to the efforts so many of you have made in the past.

When we first learned about FCC, it certainly seemed like an organization we would want to be involved in – later on. At that time, it seemed like maybe a couple of years later on. Now that I’m involved with FCC, it seems that services for waiting parents could have been and ought to be a logical extension of FCC itself – readily available to as many new prospective parents as possible. After all, who better to guide and counsel new applicants than those who have come before? I would hope that offering such services would also be a good source of new and committed members. So far I have had good positive responses from those of you with whom I have mentioned the idea so far. Any further feedback would be most appreciated.

In any event, I’d certainly like to make the creation of a waiting-parents service a personal priority for at least my first few months as co-chair. Hopefully during this time I’ll also get to meet as many of you as possible and begin learning about those things you would all like to see continue, as well as those things you would like to see evolve. So also please forward any suggestions for new services to me whenever you can (ftkenny@shaw.ca). One thought that has come up, for example, is a possible annual clothing/ equipment exchange type of thing.

Otherwise, I am looking forward to meeting many of you at the New Years celebration – assuming, of course, that David’s little Maia doesn’t somehow ‘negotiate’ me out of my ticket.
