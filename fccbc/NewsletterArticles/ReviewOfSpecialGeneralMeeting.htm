Review of Special General Meeting, May 08, 1999

© Marlee Groening, FCC BC & NW WA

On Saturday, May 8, FCC of BC held a Special General Meeting to discuss the proposed Constitution and elect a new co-chair. Eleven members, representing eight families, attended. While the attendance was not what we had hoped for, the interest and rousing debate more than made up for it.

Overall, the response to the constitution as presented was positive.Most of the comments, related to language and word usage. For example, a number of people drew attention to the need for a more inclusive language in the constitution, i.e., not all families are two parent, nor two gender.

A few of the points stimulated vigorous debates yet remain unresolved. One issue that arose involved the lack of fairness that arises from one vote for a single parent family, versus two votes for a two-parent family. Membership was another issue that surfaced, i.e., should membership include families with children from China and related organizations, or families only. An additional point that emerged was the minimum number of newsletter issues per year that FCC members’ can/should expect. This last point also highlighted a debate that has come to light since the meeting, regarding the need for an constitution in the first place.

The last point also relates to the unresolved areas of the constitution and involves the matter of how to access membership voices, in a democratic, efficient and cost-effective manner. This access is a critical process in order for organizational leaders to act on behalf and in accordance to the wishes of the membership with some degree of confidence.

As the constitution process now stands, the revised constitution will be perused by a new set of eyes in order to take the constitution to the next level, and then hopefully back to the membership for your approval. So, stay tuned.

Many thanks again, to John Bowen for all the time and effort he put into the first constitution draft.

Not to be upstaged by a constitution, was the election, by acclamation, of our new co-chair, Wanda Pierson. Congratulations Wanda, and a hearty welcome. In addition to co-chairing, Wanda will be chairing the planning committee for our upcoming Chinese New Years Party.

On a final note, a problem that surfaced at our SGM was the matter of childcare during our meetings. While we want to encourage the attendance of children, the resulting noise level rendered some of us almost incoherent. I think the time has come to either hire a couple of babysitters or have several parents volunteer to baby-sit the attending children at each meeting. Any feedback or volunteers?

Marlee Groening is Co-chair of FCC BC & NW WA
