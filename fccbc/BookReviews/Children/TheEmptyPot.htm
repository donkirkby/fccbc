The Empty Pot

by Demi. Publisher: Henry Holt and Company, New York, 1990 Canadian publisher: Fitzhenry & Whiteside Limited, Markham, Ontario

Review by Heather McDonald

This book was featured on the children's television program, "Reading Rainbow" and thus the story may be familiar to some of you. The book is written and illustrated by Demi, with exquisite drawings and a clear, simple text. The story is about a boy named Ping who lived a long time ago in China. He loved flowers and had a talent for growing them. Along with many other children in the kingdom, he enters into a contest sponsored by the Emperor, to discover who can grow the most beautiful flower. The story is a fable which emphasizes the courageous spirit that is required to be an honest person. Both my daughter and I enjoyed the beautiful illustrations and the moral of the story. Because we read and re-read this story throughout the Spring and Summer, when we too were planting flowers and waiting for the outcome of the blossoms, we could emphathize with Ping's hopeful watching and watering of his plant. Suggested age group: 2 - 6 years of age.
