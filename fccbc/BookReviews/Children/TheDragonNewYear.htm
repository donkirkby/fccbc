The Dragon New Year, a Chinese Legend

by David Bouchard, Paintings by Zhong-Yang Huang , published by Rain Coast Books, Vancouver ,1999

Reviewed by Heather McDonald, FCC BC & NWWA

This book takes the format of a wise grandmother passing on to her beloved granddaughter, a Chinese legend about the beginning of the tradition of celebrating Chinese New Year. The story weaves a tale about an evil sea dragon, the Buddha, and a grieving mother who has lost her son to the sea dragon. The qualities of courage and kindness enable the woman to overcome the dragon and make way for a better and safer year to come.

The illustrations are exquisite and the story an exciting one. It is not suitable for young children, however, as the sea dragon is indeed monstrous. This would be a suitable New Years gift for an older child. Ages 7 and up years
