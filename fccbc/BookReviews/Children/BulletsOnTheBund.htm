Bullets on the Bund

By Steve Whan, member of Families with Children from China, British Columbia

© Reviewed by Rick Waugh

Have you seen any kids' fiction with a main character that is an adoptee from China? I hadn't, up until I read Steve's book, and I'm pleased I did.

Autumn is 12 years old. With her best friend Adam, she stumbles on a mystery; a small dog wearing a back pack, running through a park after school. Autumn and Adam decide to do some detective work, in the best tradition of the Hardy Boys and Nancy Drew, and the fun begins.

Autumn is also a daydreamer and a writer. She imagines a story of a young Shanghainese girl, abandoned at birth as she was, who is caught up in the happenings of war as the Japanese are starting to invade. The tale of Qiu Yu is woven between Autumn's detective work, with nice effect.

The story is engaging, the characters well drawn. A good deal of information about Chinese culture and history, and a nicely done portrayal of some of the issues and life a Chinese girl adopted into a western family will face, are laid on with a reasonably light hand.

I haven't seen anything else like it. More books are planned.
