China: A Literary Companion

Written by A. C. Grayling and Susan Whitfield, published by John Murray Publishers, London.

Reviewed by Rick Waugh

'If China had not been there,' wrote Lord Dunsany, 'this land of dragons, peach trees, peonies and plum blossoms, with its ages of culture, slowly storing its dreams in green jade, is just the land that poets would have invented.'

This quote is just one of many included in this delightful book. The authors have pulled prose and poetry from many centuries and writers, both Chinese and foreign, to illustrate what China, its history and culture are all about. The authors discuss a point or a place; they then switch over to the writers' quotes to illustrate, maintaining a lovely balance when doing so.

The chapters have sublime titles: 'Rivers and Routes', 'The Poet in the Landscape', 'Gods, Ghosts and Demons', 'Sons of Heaven' are just a few. It's a literary book, explaining culture, and both Chinese and foreign interaction with that culture, yet it does so in a light, engaging manner. It's literary, but neither pretentious, nor tedious. It's a travelogue, but you can see and feel the people and the landscape they traverse.

Confucius wrote, (quoted in the preface to 'Pleasures'), 'To be fond of something is better than merely to know it; to find joy in it is better than merely to be fond of it.' This book will increase your joy in knowing more of the culture of your children's birth, and provide immense pleasure all by itself.
