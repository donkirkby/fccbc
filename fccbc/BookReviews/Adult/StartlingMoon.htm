Startling Moon

By Liu Hong

2001, Headline Book Publishing, Surrey, England

Reviewed by Heather Mcdonald

I noticed this book in Vancouver Airport’s bookstore. It is advertised as "an unforgettable story of a young girl’s passage to adulthood in modern China," and a book that "crosses cultural divides to touch readers everywhere."

Don’t believe the hype. I offer this review in the hope of saving you money (don’t buy this book, whatever you do!), and your time (don’t waste your time reading it). It is a boring, rambling, pointless tale with characters that never fully develop and a puzzling story line. The author can’t write, and she can’t tell a story.

Enough said.
