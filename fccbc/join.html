ABOUT THE APPLICATION FORM FOR FAMILY MEMBERSHIP

The form is necessarily long to provide for the fact that FCC is an organization of families. Families vary greatly. Some have one parent, some have two. Some have one adopted child from China, some more than one. Some families have children born to one parent or both and some have children adopted from countries other than China. All members of all member families are important to the life of the family and the life of the organization.

The motive for requesting a lot of information is to compile a database of information that families may, at their discretion, want to share with other member families. It is understood that different families will have different views about how much or how little information they want to share and with whom. It should be clear to everyone that what information they choose to share is entirely their decision and disclosure is not a condition of membership in FCC BC and NW WA.

An FCC member's control over FCC use of family information can be exercised in two ways:

All information supplied on application forms is entered into a database. This information is held in strict confidence, for the use of elected FCC representatives for FCC purposes only: Only with the express consent of the family concerned will their information be published in a members' directory. And if a family wants to share some but not all information they supply on the form, they can indicate same on the application form;

What specific information a family chooses to enter on the form is the family's choice. At an absolute minimum, it is possible for a family to join FCC BC by supplying only two pieces of information:

  1. An indication that they indeed have a child adopted in China and
  2. A mailing address for the newsletter and other mailings.

Even all names can be withheld. A number is assigned to each member family in the database, anyway.

The benefits of sharing more information are many. If people supply more ways of being contacted (eg. e-mail and fax), they are more likely to be contacted, and less likely to miss something when time is scarce. And if enough families supply enough information about the sort of variables that typically interest members, it will be possible to readily connect families with each other. The sorts of variables I mean are - age of child, neighbourhood, month and year of adoption, orphanage, family size/composition, etc.

BENEFITS OF MEMBERSHIP IN FCC BC

You will -

  • Be kept informed of FCC events and of issues of interest to all families with children adopted in China;
  • Receive the FCC BC and NW WA newsletter, Friends of the Family, free of charge;
  • Receive a directory of FCC BC members;
  • Enjoy a significant discount at all FCC events that will, in itself, make the $20 family membership fee worthwhile;
  • Ensure that your children will have a network that may prove invaluable to them throughout their lives. Without your support is will not survive.
