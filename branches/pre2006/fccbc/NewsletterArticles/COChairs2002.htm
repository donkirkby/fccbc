Co-Chairs Report Annual General meeting November 17 2001

By Wanda Pierson and Connie Munro, FCCBC & NWWA, outgoing chairs

We are pleased to present our report for the past 12 months.

Chinese New Year was again a major event for our organization. A successful lunch with entertainment was held January 20, in the auditorium of the Chinese Cultural Centre. Approximately 265 people attended.

On February 3 2001, David Robinson and Jackie Wong hosted a coffee party for waiting parents on behalf of the FCC at their home. This was done through contact with Bonnie Wong, the facilitator for many China adoptions taking place out of the Lower Mainland. A doctor, who has adopted a daughter from China, spoke about medical issues traveling parents might anticipate, and some parents whose adoptions were completed, talked about their experiences. This was part of an attempt by the FCC to be more proactive in reaching out and encouraging waiting parents participation in our organization.

In April both co-chairs met with representative of the Chinese Cultural Centre. This resulted in two new initiatives for the FCC.

We now have a Volunteer Grandparents program where Chinese seniors meet with FCC families and provide some cultural and language contact for our children. This group had their first meeting October 29. Thanks go to Lorna Hunter for guiding this project. Interested parents are encouraged to contact Lorna Hunter who is organizing this program for FCC.

The second program to evolve following the April meeting was the cultural camp for children held from July 16th to July 27th at the Chinese Cultural Centre. The childrens’ schedule involved exposure to Mandarin, calligraphy, crafts, music, puppet shows, games, making Chinese food, storytelling and various field trips. This was an ambitious undertaking by the Chinese Cultural Centre intended to benefit English speaking children of Chinese origin between the ages of 3 and 7 years. They are willing to repeat the effort in 2002, but we clearly will great more enrollment than the 16 children who attended the 2001 program if we expect the Chinese Cultural Centre to continue to provide this program. Parents are encouraged to complete the survey regarding culture camps, included with their Autumn newsletter, and return it to Wanda Pierson.

On July 8, the FCC held a successful summer picnic at Ryall Park in New Westminster. The park’s spray pool, toddler area and climbing web proved very popular. The picnic was moved from June to July this year in an effort to capture better weather. Thanks go to Kathy Coleman for her work in chairing the Summer Picnic committee.

Part of the role of the FCC executive is to field questions from persons contemplating adoption from China. Several informal discussions and meetings with prospective adoptive parents took place during the year. On June 23, Wanda met with a waiting parents group, arranged by the Ministry of Children and Family Development. On November 7, Connie met with a waiting parents group, all dealing with Sunrise Agency and who have commenced regular meetings in a space at the Vancouver Public Library.

The executive held pot luck dinner meetings on February 10 and May 12 to get to know each other better and to supplement their usual communication which is largely conducted by email.

Both of us, Connie and Wanda are stepping down as co-chairs at the November 17 Annual General Meeting. We wish the new executive well.
