A Visit from A Kindred Spirit

Copyright © John Bowen

Those of you who were able to meet Amy Klatzkin on January 15, at the CCC, found in her, a kindred spirit. Amy is an FCC mom who lives in San Francisco. She is also the editor of San Francisco’s FCC newsletter and the editor of the marvelous book, “A Passage to the Heart: Writings from Families with Children from China” (see review and purchase details in this issue of FOF).

Amy came to Vancouver to talk about local FCC’s working together for the common good of all families and for the children we left behind in Chinese orphanages. I know from a long-standing email acquaintance with Amy that she was keen to visit Vancouver, the other really Chinese city in North America.

Amy introduced her presentation with an overview of Adoption in China in the 1990s, the text of which follows. We saw a video which showed conditions in Chinese orphanages, devastated by recent floods and improvements that have made possible by FCC’s in the United States. I realized, more than ever, that we in BC need to do more to help the orphans left behind. We are presently exploring options and you will hear more from us in future on this subject. In the mean time, there is a sure-fire way you can help kids in orphanages. Buy a copy of “A Passage to the Heart”. The publisher of the book, Yeong and Yeong, is generously donating 10% of sales proceeds directly to orphanages in China to improve the lives of children housed there.
