Changes to Chinese Adoption Laws

By Bonny Wong

On November 4, 1998, the National People’s Congress of the people’s Republic of China passed amendments to the existing Adoption Law of the People’s Republic of China. The amended Adoption Law came into force April 1, 1999. Here, I will only discuss the amendments, which are relevant to foreign applicants applying for non-relative adoption.

Both Chinese citizens and foreign applicants are covered by Article 6 of the Adoption Law:

“Adopters shall meet simultaneously the following requirements:

  1. childless
  2. capable of rearing and educating the adoptee
  3. no illness which is deemed medically as inappropriate for the adopter to adopt children
  4. having reached the age of 30”

However, foreign applicants may adopt a child or twins from a social welfare institute (often referred to as orphanage) “irrespective of the restrictions that the adopter shall be childless and adopt one child only” (Article 8). Please note that twins are not always available for adoption.

The adopted child/children may be free from any special need or handicap condition. China cannot of course guarantee the health of any child, but there is no longer a requirement to adopt a “special needs” child if you do not meet requirements. However, you can still adopt a special needs child if you so desire.

The minimum age of foreign applicants has been reduced from 35 to 30. No applicant under 30 years old can adopt any type of child.

Another amendment is the effective date of the adoption is changed from the date of notarization to the date of registration (Article 15).

Bonnie Wong runs Tribo Services, a consultation agency facilitating adoptions from China.
