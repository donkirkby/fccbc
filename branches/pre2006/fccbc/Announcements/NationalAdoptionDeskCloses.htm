National Adoption Desk Closes

The National Adoption Desk (NAD), a federal institute for over 20 years, is in the process of being dismantled. The Desk, a part of the Department of Human Resource Development in Ottawa was organized at the request of the provinces (except Quebec) and the territories to handle intercountry adoption information, agreements, and files. Since that time, the NAD has provided a consistent interface between the provinces and other countries.

Starting August 1, 2000 the NAD will begin disengaging from case management. In other words, individual family files will no longer be required to go through the NAD. China, in response to this phase out will accredit the licensed adoption agencies.

As of November 1st, the licensed adoption agencies will be responsible for sending the China document package directly the Chinese authorities (China Centre for Adoption Affairs or CCAA).

The NAD's future is uncertain at this time. As of March 2001, they will be completely disengaged from case management. They will be replaced by the Child, Family and Community (CFC) division of Human Resources Development Canada and apparently will take on a role of policy development. Further information on the CFC can be obtained by visiting their website.
