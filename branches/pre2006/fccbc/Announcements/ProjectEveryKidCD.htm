PROJECT EVERYKID CD

Four years ago, as my husband Eric and I were in family planning mode, I also was planning to get involved in some community work. This is how it would work: Said husband and I would I complete all paper work to adopt our baby from China (one quiet and happy baby -OK, we requested twins but never thought...) I would write a proposal for funding to the Grizzlies Foundation. I would receive the funding and have one child-free year to get the program up and running. We then would travel to China, scoop up our baby girl and return home. Sounds perfect, right?

The likelihood of everything working out as I dreamed is, well, as likely as my children standing at attention every time the telephone rings! . Instead, Eric and I travelled to China in August, 98 where we met our twin 14 month old daughters , Olivia Mei-Li and Maggie Qian-Bo (did I mention "active and outgoing" daughters?). We were home not more than four weeks when the call came: "are you still looking for funding?". Amidst the heaps of happy baby sounds, I muttered (that's how sleep deprived mothers talk) "of course I'm still interested."

Timing aside, I'm proud to announce that all my dreams came true. Our first project, Songs in the Key of Culture, is now for sale. Our non-profit society, Project EveryKid Foundation, is dedicated to providing after school programs in schools that need them. The EveryKid Choir, students from Norquay Elementary in East Vancouver, is 25 of the sweetest sounding kids you'll ever hear. ASZA, four multi-talented musicians, created the rich and rhythmic accompaniment you will enjoy. Proceeds from the sale of the CDs fund future after school programs.

Songs in the Key of Culture is made up of 14 songs from around the world: Canada, China, Vietnam, Japan, Iran, the Philippines, Ireland, and the Puget Sound area. The songs are performed by the EveryKid Choir, 25 elementary school students from Vancouver BC. and their mentors, seven high school students from Windermere Secondary. The lyrics are sung in the language of origin first, followed by English. This is intelligent music for children and families.

ASZA is four musicians from different cultures who weave their traditions into an exotic blend of rich and powerful music. Using instruments from around the world, they combine the techniques and rhythms of many musical cultures. The result is a whirlwind of styles moving through a powerful mosaic of sound.

So gather the family around the CD player and get ready to start dancing and singing! This is a collection of music to be enjoyed by all ages and cultures.

The cost is $15.00. You can purchase a CD through the AFA library or by calling Linda McDaniel at (604) 431-5057 or email her.
