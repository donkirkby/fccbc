ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING - 2003

FCC BC & NWW will hold its Annual General Meeting on November 8th at Century House, New Westminster. We will briefly review what's been happening with< FCC and discuss our draft 10 Year Vision, future programs/events and what we< envisage for the future. There will be a short presentation about a mentor program under discussion with Success between our families and Asian families, as well as a presentation about a project to match Big Sister volunteers of Chinese heritage, language, and culture (University age), with our Chinese-born adoptees.

We will introduce Cynthia Xie, an intercultural communications expert from China, who has volunteered to help us develop Chinese cultural programs for our members and their children.

We have a Chinese clown for the children (details of the clown are below) and hope also to be showing the children some fun Chinese DVD cartoons popular in China.

Tea, coffee and snacks will be available for members and their children.

Date: November 8, 2003 from 2 till 4pm (Please arrive at least 10 minutes early so we can get started on time)

Place: Century House
620, 8th Street
New Westminster
(Parking is available in their parking lot, otherwise street parking is available nearby)

How can you participate?

* Elect 2 new Co-chairs
* Help us decide on future programs for our children
* Tell us what you would like to see FCC doing in the future
* Socialise with other families
* Sign up to help on any of our events/programs below:

1) Waiting Families
2) Older Children
3) Chinese New Year
4) Jane Brown's Playshops
5) Summer picnic
6) Dimsum and other restaurant get togethers
7) Newsletter
6) Cultural events
9) Others

We will have sign-up sheets available -- please consider donating a little of your time to these great causes but do n't feel you have to! We appreciate that time is scarce for most families, but sometimes a little help goes a long way and who knows you might enjoy it and make new friends in the bargain. Thank you!

Are you interested in becoming a Board Member? It is a very rewarding experience. Not only do you get to help plan upcoming events and promote the Chinese culture, but you also have the opportunity to expand your network of friends and associates. Regular meetings are held at the Executive

Members' homes usually followed by pot luck.

The Magician


Ray started magic at the age of eleven by working in a magic shop after
school. He worked for the McDonald's Restaurant Corporation for five years
performing eight shows a week for their parties.


In addition to his formative years in the art of magic, Ray took three years
of private ballroom dancing lessons, was a member of Toastmasters
International and studied with some of Canada's top Theatresports
improvisors and comedians.


Ray has been dazzling his audiences for the past 18 years with his unique
blend of mysteries from the Orient, baffling effects and hilarious comedy.
He has a gentle way with children, entertaining them at private parties, and
together with his engaging and charismatic personality, Ray is a favourite
at corporate adult functions and family events. His trademark "Orange"
routine is one of the funniest, most amazing effects you will ever see!


He is a member of two major magic organizations and is a past President of
Vancouver's oldest magic club, the Vancouver Magic Circle. Ray is a
recipient of the club's "Wilf Rutherford Trophy" awarded for the best
children's magic show, and the "Cecil Ackery Trophy" awarded for the best
comedy magic show.


Today, Ray makes his home in Vancouver, Canada, and has performed hundreds
of shows for all occasions.
