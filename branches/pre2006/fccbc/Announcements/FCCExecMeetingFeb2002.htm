MINUTES OF FCC & NWW EXECUTIVE MEETING - February 2002

Held February 23, 2002 at 1357 Crestwell Rd., West Vancouver

Present:

David Robinson Co-chair
Frank Kenny Co-chair
Cathy McDormand Treasurer
Rick Waugh Webmaster|
Kathy Stewart Newsletter Editor
Peter Gibson Membership
Danielle Soulodre Organizer of CNY
Steve Whan Guest
Regrets: Heather McDonald

The previous Minutes were reviewed & discussed. Cathy McDormand reported that there was a roughly $400.00 profit from this year’s New Year’s event, with $120.00 in expenses still to come. Few feedback forms were completed. Rick to look at putting feedback form on website. Danielle has kindly agreed to organize next year’s event. She requested FCC purchase an urn for coffee and will supply an estimate. It was agreed that there needed to be some discussion about venue and activities but that this should be postponed until the next meeting. David reported that he had been in touch with hotels in Richmond with banquet halls as possible future venues. The Richmond Inn offered good facilities (the hall is free when large numbers attend) but can only be booked 4 months in advance (CCC have the same booking policy for their hall). To get a quote for food, we have to provide numbers of adults and children plus ages of latter; Cathy/Danielle will provide details from this year’s event, so we can get an estimate from Richmond Inn.

Also arising from the previous minutes was the issue of a Culture Camp. John Bowen has reported having had no responses on his request for feedback on interest and feels he cannot proceed further. David thought that Wanda might be contacted about her interest in pursuing CCC to see if they can be persuaded to set something up soon so that our members can be notified. Danielle will ask Wanda. The summer picnic is to be held on July 7 and promises to be a success as in previous years. David & Camille are to organize a second summer event at Cultas Lake and will put details on the website and discussion line once they have a date. June or August are the likely dates.

David spoke on the need to have activities for older children. There was much discussion of this. Frank has yet to get information from RVYC about a weekend cruise to Wigwam Inn. However, there was some concern expressed about age. David thought the ‘older’ children would include those 6 – 11. Frank thought a cruise might be appropriate only for 8 yr olds and up. Further discussion suggested that 6 – 8 year olds still might be interested in the New Year’s event if more appropriate activities were planned for them. This issue needs to be discussed further.

David suggested that a few times a year, say the first Sunday of a month, FCC members could drop in at a dimsum restaurant in China Town, where a back room could be reserved. This would give members and waiting parents opportunity to meet others. This may also appeal to parents with older children. This suggestion will be put on the discussion line to see if members express interest in it. Other suggestions included arranging talks on adoption issues and arranging a soccer game between older children and adults.

Frank raised the issue of providing services to waiting families – specifically setting up resource groups for them. There have been difficulties in locating waiting families via the agencies and in coordination. Various proposals were entertained & discussed. It was decided that the best approach would be to hold 3 afternoon or evening teas a year to which waiting families could be invited by publicity provided through the agencies/AFABC and our website. Individuals coming to those teas could be then organized into resource groups if they wished. It was thought that these should take place in different venues, such as Century House. It was agreed that FCC would defray the cost of these, since it was fairly low (i.e., $66.00 for Century House). Frank proposed, and it was agreed, that new member Morgan Armstrong would be responsible for this service (if acceptable to her) and should also sit on the executive. She will be invited to the next meeting.

Peter reported that membership had risen to 117 families. A new directory of members is now out.

David also stressed the importance of encouraging members in their localities to organize events such as a picnic and games in a park, meeting at a restaurant, getting a Chinese teacher for a group of kids. This was member families in a locality get to know one another and bonds can be made.

It was agreed that agencies should be invited to advertise in the newsletter. Any advert would have to be in the Newsletter for the year or balance of the year and the cost would have to be paid in advance. Kathy will send letters to the agencies. Agreed that Family members could advertise for free but the Editor will retain discretion on what can go in.

The next Executive meeting will be April 27 at 3pm at the home of Danielle Soulodre & Steve Whan at 2730 Cresentview Drive, North Vancouver. 604-987-9852.

The meeting was followed by a very nice pot luck supper. Frank’s chili was wonderful (spectacular would be a better term), David mistook the dogs’ food for his but did not appear to notice the difference (and so the dogs went without supper). Danielle served some delicious suchi and gave an impromtu lesson on how to make California Rolls. The children got balloons and a cake with smarties. No money was found in the cake. Missing in Action: David’s Curry – although we did get a feeble excuse about botched dates.(or was it because he did not want to blow Frank’s socks off?)
