Medical Accuracy Survey

Medical information is needed from families who have already adopted children from China to assist physicians evaluating this information to examine its accuracy and predictive value. This informal study seeks to look at the information provided on the referral from China (particularly length of the child, weight, and head size), comparing it to the growth statistics after the child arrives home. When examining referral information from China, there is very little objective data provided.

Examining this data (using the Chinese growth chart) will allow physicians to monitor if this information is useful to families. If interested, please send your child's date of birth, province, any measurements shared on the referral (with dates), and any growth measurements after arrival, particularly at the first doctor visit, with the date of arrival and date of first visit. Laboratory data, both before and after arrival, will also be useful. Please also enclose a note that this data may be used for research purposes. Children will not be identified by name.
