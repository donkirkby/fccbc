Update from the Mills Family May 17, 2000

Kimberley's in the ring again!

This time, however, in her corner, is --- radiation. A recent scan indicates little or no shrinkage of the tumour as a result of the latest chemotherapy treatment, of which she had two rounds. When we met with the oncologist at the end of April, he said since the initial scan (in November/99) there, perhaps, has been a very slight shrinkage of the tumour. Since the difference is negligible, the doctors feel to continue with chemotherapy would do more harm than good. (At least the tumour hasn't grown!) So, the plan is to give radiation a whirl. Kimmy will undergo 16 treatments at the B.C. Cancer Agency in Vancouver. She underwent her first treatment Monday, May 15th. Based on 5 days on and 2 off, Kimberley should have her final session on June 6th or 7th. Perhaps, after her treatment, she can attend the final two weeks of school. It should also be towards the end of June that we will determine the results of the radiation treatment.

We've touched somewhat on other options, with the oncologist, such as traveling to Tijuana, Mexico where some experimental treatments are available. We also spoke about including Kimberley in human clinical trials of "metronomic dosing", the new method that made the news a month or two ago, whereby small, but constant doses of chemo are given, in addition to drugs that prevent the growth of blood vessels, which the tumour needs to feed off of. The oncologist thinks Kim would be a good candidate for that. There are so many other possibilities, albeit experimental ones. We have to keep in mind that Kim's tumour is very, very rare and the doctors have nothing to compare it to. In the meantime, one at a time, one bridge at a time. Hopefully, the radiation won't make Kimmy too sick.

Her spirits continue to soar and she remains energetic. She's even gone to school for a few days. The tooth fairy visited her a couple times lately, too, although one night she forgot, but we think that more than forgetting, she simply had to go back to bring more money (because it was a special tooth) and that took time. So, it was the following night that the tooth fairy arrived with a wheelbarrow-full of money, or so it seemed.

Kimmy doesn't know it yet, but she's going to the upcoming N Sync concert at Vancouver' s GM Place in June. She loves music ... singing and dancing and nothing makes her happier. So she'll be thrilled with knowing she's going to the concert. We'll save the news as a surprise towards the end of her radiation treatment. We'll look forward to giving good medical news on the next update!!

David O. Mills
