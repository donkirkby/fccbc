A Study Exploring the Transition to Single Adoptive Motherhood

This study seeks to explore the transition for single women who decide to adopt a child. The goal of the research is to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the life transition process leading to single female parent adoption.

The researcher, Sandra Chopping, is interested in talking to single women who have adopted a child at mid-life (40 - 55 years of age) within the last 5 years and have been a single adoptive parent for a minimum of one year. Participation in the study involves:

  • discussing your life experiences which are part of the transition process to becoming a single adoptive mother with a female researcher on one or two occasions.
  • reading and commenting on a summary of your experiences and on the final presentation of the data.
  • a total participation time of approximately 3 hours.

This masters thesis research is being conducted under the supervision of Dr. W. Borgen, Dept. of Counseling psychology at the University of British Columbia. Participation in this study will be completely confidential. Reimbursement for baby-sitting costs during the interview time is available if required.

To participate or to gain further information about this research study, please contact Sandra Chopping (M.A. Candidate) by telephone (263-9450) or by e-mail at chopping@interchange.ubc.ca, or Dr. W. Borgen, Research Supervisor at 822-5259. Thank you for your interest in this project
