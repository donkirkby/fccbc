Questions About Adopting in China?

FCC BC is devoted to providing a community for families who have adopted children from China. It is a support organization, a group of people your family can turn to for questions, help, and someone else who has been through, or is going through, the same issues as you.

We also like to provide help for those thinking about adopting from China. However, we are not the best repository of information on how to adopt, what the rules are, issues and costs.

If you are seriously thinking about adopting in China, the place to start is the Adoptive Families Association of British Columbia. Visit their web site for information about contacting them. They have an extensive library of research material, both on paper and on video. They have all the latest information on government regulations, and an idea as to general costs. They run regular informational seminars on the issues of adopting from a foreign country. They can also help you compare foreign adoption from various countries, local adoption, and local special needs adoption.

However, if you want to talk to someone who has adopted from China, because you feel strongly that this might be the route for you, send us an e-mail. Someone will contact you shortly.

We do not generally entertain requests for information or participation in studies, papers, articles, or documentaries. We have seen a number of these requests come through over the years, and rarely pass them onto our members.
