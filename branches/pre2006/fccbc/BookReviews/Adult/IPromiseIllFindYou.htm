I Promise I'll Find You

By Heather Patricia ward, art by Sheila McGraw, published by Firefly Books Ltd.

Reviewed by Heather McDonald, FCC BC & NWWA

This book does not, on it's face, have a Chinese theme or relate to adoption in any way.

The book is dedicated " with love to all the missing children in the world and to the memory of Kelly Cook". A portion of the author's proceeds from the sale of the book will benefit child-locating agencies.

I knew none of this when I first read the book, having borrowed it from the library to read to my daughter.

Immediately the poetry caught me. For me, it resurrected a flood of emotion as I recalled how much I wanted my daughter, and how I was prepared to search the ends of the earth to find her. I knew she was waiting for me, somewhere, and somehow, no matter what the obstacles, I was going to find her and bring her home.

I ended up purchasing the book, more for me than for Julia, I think. The rhymes are special to me:

"And if I had no other way
  I'd walk or crawl or run
I'd search to the ends of the earth
  For you my precious one".

Age group: 0 ? infinity

Heather McDonald is a member of FCC BC & NWWA and is a frequent contributor of book reviews.
