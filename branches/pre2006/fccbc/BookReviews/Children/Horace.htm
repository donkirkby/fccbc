Horace

by Holly Keller, Publisher: Mulberry Booksm, ISBN 0-688-09831-2

© by Margaret Patton - Victoria BC

This Reading Rainbow book is written for the pre-school age child. It deals simply and beautifully with the issue of looking different from one's family members.

The story is about Horace, a leopard, who was adopted by tigers. Horace has a happy life with his parents and every night at bedtime his mother tells him how they chose him when he was a tiny baby. She also tells him how they liked his spots and wanted him for their child. But Horace always falls asleep before the last part of the story.

As Horace grows he becomes more aware of being different and eventually runs away in search of a family who looks like him. He finds some leopard friends and has a wonderful day, but as evening falls he longs for home. When his mother and father find him he is very happy to return home with them.

That night Horace finally hears all of his mother's story and adds his own special ending.

The story and illustrations in this book are simple and charming and the story is heart-warming. I recommend it to any of you facing this issue with your child. I had to special order the book from my local bookstore.
