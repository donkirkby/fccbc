Chinese New Year

By Tricia Brown, photographs by Fran Oritz, published by Henry Holt and Co.; New York, 1987

Reviewed by Heather McDonald, FCC BC & NWWA

This small paperback was a "Notable Children's Trade Book in the Field of Social Studies". It is an informative introduction to Chinese New Year customs and traditions, with black and white photos which compliment the text. The focus is on American cities and the way in which Chinese New Year is currently celebrated in North America. It has an objective, school text-book style that does not attempt to tell a personal story; perhaps for that reason it is not one of my favourites to read to my child. However, I would recommend it as a worthwhile addition to a child's library as it gives a good basic background to the meaning of Chinese New Year customs. Ages 3 years and up.
